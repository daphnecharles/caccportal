A.     Common Fixes

·        Please note: NetOps is now certified to repair Xerox 5500, N4525, and 6350 Printers.

·        Restarting the Printer can solve 50% of problems.

·        Checking physical connections is always a good idea.

·        Patrons accidentally press the "Pause" / Go buttons frequently.

·        Always check pickup rollers and fusers whenever the printers jam.

·        A good cleaning will keep the repair/cleaning supervisor happy.

·        If it is absolutely necessary to remove a printer from queue, please be sure to include an E-Mail to cacc_lab stating why it was taken out of rotation.

B.     Hardware

·        Xerox Color Phaser 6250N

1.      Paper Jams (There are three common locations for paper jams on the 6250N: the Transfer Roller, the Imaging Unit, and the Fuser.)

a.       Transfer Roller

                                                                                                                                       i.      Simply press button A on the right side of the printer and slowly lower the casing until it goes no further.

                                                                                                                                     ii.      You should then be able to see (and remove) the jammed paper.

b.      Imaging Unit

                                                                                                                                       i.      The Imaging Unit rests above the Transfer Roller.

                                                                                                                                     ii.      If pages get jammed there you must first get access to the Transfer Roller as directed above.

                                                                                                                                    iii.      Then you must press button C on the top of the printer and lift it up.

                                                                                                                                   iv.      After that you may have to pull the Imaging Unit out to reach the jammed paper.

c.       Fuser

                                                                                                                                       i.      The Fuser is the most common location, and is accessible by pressing the B key and sliding the body down.

                                                                                                                                     ii.      The paper should then be visible.

                                                                                                                                    iii.      Please note that when working with the 6250N, the paper tray should be removed prior to opening the printer, as it tends to jam again if you open it without it being removed.

2.      Toner Related Issues

a.       The 6350N uses 4 separate toner cartridges, following the CMYK color format. If any one of these cartridges goes empty, the printer will stop working (it will report which cartridge is empty however).

b.      The cartridges are made of flimsy, weak plastic. This makes the cartridges prone to leaking, not only during removal/installation, but also during standard operation.

c.       The heads on the printer must be cleaned up weekly to prevent colors from mixing.

d.      To remove the cartridge, you must push the tabs on both sides of the cartridge up until it clicks into the unlocked position. This closes the shutter on the toner chamber and allows the cartridge to be removed.

e.       To install a new cartridge, simply slide it into place and push the tabs down until they click in the locked position.

3.      Maintenance Kit Issues

a.       If the fuser and/or imager reach the end of their life cycle, the printer will stop functioning properly. The fuser/imager will need to be replaced if this happens.

b.      If you replace the fuser, no special tasks need to be done.

c.       If the imager is replaced, the printer system must be re-calibrated.

                                                                                                                                       i.      Press OK on the Menus selection.

                                                                                                                                     ii.      Press the Down key 5 times (Support Menu selected) and press OK.

                                                                                                                                    iii.      Press the Down key 3 times (Improve Print Quality? selected) and press OK.

                                                                                                                                   iv.      Press the Down key 6 times (Color Registration Menu selected) and press OK.

                                                                                                                                     v.      Follow the instructions on the page that is printed out. 

·        Xerox N4525

1.      Paper Jam Issues

a.       There are four common locations for paper jams on the N4525.

b.      The first set is behind door A on the left side of the printer.

c.       This houses the fuser and exit rollers (which are behind the fuser).

d.      If paper is jammed in the fuser, you can use the green lever on the right of it to push the paper along until you can grab an edge.

e.       If the paper is stuck behind the fuser at the exit rollers, the fuser must be removed by loosening the two screws on the sides of it.

f.        The second set is behind door B, on the bottom left side of the printer.

g.       This houses one of the transfer rollers from the paper trays.

h.       The paper will be very visible if it is jammed here.

i.         The third location is in the toner area, behind the front door.

j.        Be very careful when moving the toner cartridge, as toner tends to leak with even the gentlest touch.

k.      Finally, there are additional rollers housed in the paper trays. Be sure to check there.

l.         Please note: The N4525 printers have been known to produce phantom jam errors, reporting paper jams when there are none. Just open doors A and B and close them, the printer will resume working.

2.      Toner Issues

a.       Do NOT shake the N4525 toners, even when they are reporting being low; shaking these toners will result in a cloud of black toner smoke being ejected from the cartridge.

b.      When the toner is empty, the printer will refuse to print, regardless of what you do (short of changing the toner).

3.      Maintenance Kit Issues

a.       The fuser generally needs to be replaced after 6 months to 1 year of use (generally 400,000 pages).

b.      The printer will commonly report on a daily basis that you should, "Replace Maintenance Kit." This is not needed unless print quality is affected (most notably, VERY frequent paper jams at the rollers or fused toner being removed by simple touching of paper).

c.       Replacing the fuser and rollers is a simple process, and the maintenance kit contains detailed instructions (and the necessary tools) to change the parts.

·        Kyocera Printers

1.      All repair beyond printer jams and toner changes is handled by a Kyocera representative.

2.      Jams tend to occur in either print trays; this can be resolved by opening the left doors and pulling out both trays completely then removing the jammed paper.

3.      Also jams can occur in the left door, as with the other printers, check the rollers and clean the printer as best as possible.

4.      DO NOT shake Kyocera toners unless they are first placed in the box tops we use for scrap paper, this will keep you from spilling toner all over yourself and the lab.

5.      CALL SERVICE ERRORS generally mean there is too much paper in the tray, remove some, turn the printer off for two minutes, then turn it back on.

D.     Print Release Stations / Pharos Remote

·        General Troubleshooting Tips

1.      If you get a complaint from a user, the first thing you should do is test it out yourself. Try logging into a machine and try to print something. Then try to release it. Remember that Guest users and some others will not have the ability to use the Release Station. They might go up to the station trying to find their job, but it has already printed out on a printer.

2.      If there is a problem with the Release Station, you can always release/move the jobs via Pharos Remote. Don't be afraid to reboot the Release Station. Just hit the power button on the front (just once, don't hold it down) and it will shut down Windows. Then hit it again to power it back up. If it doesn't load the release station software, then there might be a problem and an emergency ticket should be created. It will give an error describing why it can't load. Please note this in the ticket.

3.      Try printing to a different queue and see if you can release the job from the associated Release Station.

4.      Try waiting a few minutes. If there is a network glitch, it might take some time to reconnect. If you swipe a card and it says "communication error" or "Trying to contact DBServer," then give it some time. Call a lab on another campus to see if they are affected as well. This will allow you to determine if it's a campus-wide problem or if it's local to your lab. If it persists for more than 2-3 minutes, submit an emergency ticket.

5.      If the print release station hangs on "Reconfiguring...please wait," then reboot it. It won't take longer than 2-3 seconds to reconfigure. If it hangs, that means there's a problem and it needs to be rebooted.
